In the world of Agile project management, good communication is what allows for team success. Agile methods, which are all about being flexible and responsive, have changed the way we traditionally think about project communication. Unlike traditional project management methodologies, Agile emphasizes adaptability, collaboration, and a diversity of skill sets. Agile’s dynamic strategy of project management creates an environment with a variety of perspectives and high-quality standards. The diverse perspectives of team members should serve to contribute to the collaborative process, not take away from it. Ensuring that these perspectives are collaborative is part of establishing communication management in your project team.

In the context of Agile project management, effective communication is crucial for the success of the project. Here are several considerations within the realm of Project Communications Management in an Agile environment:

Face-to-Face Communication

Traditional project management methodologies generally utilize formal documentation and scheduled meetings, with a much lower emphasis on frequent face-to-face communication. Agile methodologies prioritize face-to-face communication. Collaboration is encouraged, and teams often hold daily stand-up meetings.

The emphasis on face-to-face communication is not something that is beneficial in traditional project management methodologies. Agile methodologies should be utilized for projects that have flexibility in the timeline and requirements, as that allows for more opportunities for collaboration.

User Stories

https://www.productplan.com/uploads/2019/01/user-story-1024x536.png

Agile projects often use user stories as a primary means of communication between the product owner and the development team. These stories are short, informal, and written in a language that both technical and non-technical team members can understand. The emphasis that Agile teams put on communication creates an environment where collaboration thrives.

Adaptive Documentation

Agile values quality and working software over comprehensive documentation. Documentation is typically lightweight, and Agile teams leave room for adaptation. On the other hand, traditional methodologies often involve extensive upfront documentation, with a focus on detailed project plans, requirements, and other formal documents. Traditional methodologies may prove more practical for basic projects, designed to be developed in an efficient manner.

Project Sprints

Communication in Agile is iterative and incremental, with frequent opportunities for feedback and adjustment. Regular sprint reviews and retrospectives create an environment for continuous improvement. Frequent team check-ins are essential for a thriving project environment that focuses on the development of project quality.

Traditional projects may have longer development cycles, with formal reviews and adjustments at major milestones rather than continuous iterations. With longer development cycles, adjustments to the project's scope may prove to be impossible. Traditional project development forces your project scope and project requirements to remain rigid.

Cross-Functional Teams

https://www.pedco.eu/2.0/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/word-image-36.png

Agile teams should be cross-functional with members possessing diverse skills. Communication needs to be collaborative, with team members sharing knowledge and working together to achieve common goals. Given the diversity of skill sets in an agile team, collaboration is essential to incorporate a variety of perspectives and insights.

Traditional projects generally will have more specialized roles, leading to communication barriers in certain areas. Without the incorporation of diverse skill sets, your team is more likely to run into conflicts that may be out of their area of expertise. However, specialized roles create a work environment that focuses on efficiency. Traditional projects generally thrive off of this efficiency.

Stakeholder Engagement

All project management methodologies require frequent stakeholder engagement. However, it is of the utmost importance when working with an Agile team. Although the methodology we are discussing is called “Agile”, you should often expect the project to have an extensive timeline. Through the constant changes of direction that occur throughout the development of the project, stakeholders may become frustrated. Whether it is from the increment to the timeline, or the direction that the development team decided to go, stakeholders need to have a sense of control over what is happening with the project. Without a strict timeline or project requirements, it can be almost impossible to envision an accurate timeline for a project. Stakeholders should be involved in sprint reviews and have opportunities to provide feedback throughout the development process. Generally, the development team will have a decent idea of the project timeline. After each sprint iteration, the entire development team should be on the same page as far as the timeline goes.

Conclusion

Collaboration and communication are fostered by the many tools that project managers utilize. Creating an environment where collaboration is encouraged is just the beginning of your work as a project manager. The extensiveness of agile development really can wear out a development team. With a team of motivated workers diversified by their skill sets, your project has a great chance of succeeding through collaboration. As a project manager, your role should be to ensure your team is efficiently communicating and collaborating.
